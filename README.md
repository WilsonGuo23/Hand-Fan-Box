# Hand-Fan-Box

## Summary

This box converts the circular motion of a motor in angular motion, which is then used to wave a hand fan (or any other object you can attach to the lever). Speed is selectable via the knob on the fan controller. Connect to any usb outlet or power supply.

## Parts required to assemble

> **NOTE:** The .SLDPRT files can be opened in a cad program of your choice and edited before being exported. Certain openings may be adjusted by 0.3-0.5 milimeters to account to tolerances related to 3d printing, if you are using a more or less accurate medium you will likely want to adjust these values. You may also resize the .stl files in your slicer if you are using a 3D printer. the PRINT_ME_STLs folder will have the stl files if you do not want to go through and export each part from your CAD program of choice.

## How to Assemble

1. Insert a ball bearing in the mounting bracket ball bearing cover.

![Fanbox_Step_1](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/4a84bf9e-7d58-4ba7-9e9b-702addf22c48)

2. Use 2 2x10mm and 2 bolts Screws to attach the mounting bracket ball bearing cover to the mounting bracket, then attach the motor to the mounting bracket with 3 M3x30mm screws and 3 bolts on the opposite side.

![Fanbox_Step_2_1](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/6f0bdc70-beca-4614-93fa-4cf9a3a82228)

![Fanbox_Step_2_2](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/aa807e13-c14a-42c4-8bb0-a27223137dd8)

3. Attach the fan box cover first half to the mounting bracket on the side of the motor using 4 M3x35mm screws and 4 M3x10mm coupling nuts.



4. Attach the motor controller to the fan motor back panel with the adjustment knob facing outwards. You will use 4 M3x16mm screws, 4 PCB spacers, and 4 bolts. Then screw in the USB passthrough on the opposite side and plug the USB power cable into the USB passthrough.

> **NOTE:** it may also be worth stripping the included USB power cable shorter to avoid clearance issues down the line

![Fanbox_Step_4](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/6198aaca-ab24-4d69-bade-59d84382ddba)

5. Slide the motor controller control knob into the fan cover second half so that the 4 screw holes are parallel and the rocker switch gap is facing away from the fan motor back plate. Place 4 M3x35mm screws in the screw holes connecting the back panel to the cover second half. Then place the rocker switch in the gap at the top of the fan cover second half. 

![Fanbox_Step_5](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/b43652b5-6e6a-4610-8df2-638d2529ec6e)

6. Strip the motor power wires and screw them into the appropriate screw terminals. Power the machine to be sure that the wiring is done correctly. Place the 2 fan cover halves together and twist in the 4 screws on the back panel side.

![Fanbox_Step_6](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/6b19402b-7c37-4852-b1b7-be2c9a517f7f)

7. Place the motor to ball bearing connector on the motor and insert the bottom of the fan lever into the ball bearing held in place by the ball bearing cover.
