# Hand-Fan-Box

## Summary

This box converts the circular motion of a motor into angular motion, which is then used to wave a hand fan (or any other object you can attach to the lever). The speed can be adjusted using the knob on the fan controller. Connect to any USB outlet or power supply. Note that USB does not transfer enough power to lift objects heavier than a few grams.

## Parts required to assemble

- Manufacture the parts included in the PRINT_ME folder. All parts are single pieces except for the Ball Bearing to Ball Bearing Connector, which requires two, and the PCB Spacer, which requires four.

> **NOTE:** The .SLDPRT files can be opened in a cad program of your choice and edited before being exported. Certain openings may be adjusted by 0.3-0.5 millimeters to account for tolerances related to 3d printing, if you are using a more or less accurate medium you will likely want to adjust these values. You may also resize the .stl files in your slicer if you are using a 3D printer. the PRINT_ME_STLs folder will have the stl files if you do not want to go through and export each part from your CAD program of choice.

## How to Assemble

1. Insert a ball bearing into the mounting bracket ball bearing cover.

![Fanbox_Step_1](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/4a84bf9e-7d58-4ba7-9e9b-702addf22c48)

2. Use 2 M3x10mm screws and 2 bolts to attach the mounting bracket ball bearing cover to the mounting bracket, then attach the motor to the mounting bracket with 3 M3x30mm screws and 3 bolts on the opposite side.

![Fanbox_Step_2_1](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/6f0bdc70-beca-4614-93fa-4cf9a3a82228)

![Fanbox_Step_2_2](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/aa807e13-c14a-42c4-8bb0-a27223137dd8)

3. Attach the fan box cover first half to the mounting bracket on the side of the motor using 4 M3x35mm screws and 4 M3x10mm coupling nuts.

![Fanbox_Step_3](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/5a37b16a-c8df-45bc-a171-8313be2f902e)

4. Attach the motor controller to the fan motor back panel with the adjustment knob facing outward. You will use 4 M3x16mm screws, 4 PCB spacers, and 4 bolts. Then screw in the USB passthrough on the opposite side and plug the USB power cable into the USB passthrough.

> **NOTE:** It may also be worth cutting the included USB power cable shorter to avoid clearance issues later.

![Fanbox_Step_4](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/6198aaca-ab24-4d69-bade-59d84382ddba)

5. Slide the motor controller control knob into the fan cover second half so that the four screw holes are aligned and the rocker switch gap is facing away from the fan motor back plate. Place 4 M3x35mm screws in the screw holes connecting the back panel to the cover second half. Then place the rocker switch in the gap at the top of the fan cover second half. 

![Fanbox_Step_5](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/b43652b5-6e6a-4610-8df2-638d2529ec6e)

6. Strip the motor power wires and screw them into the appropriate screw terminals. Power the device briefly to ensure the wiring is correct. Place the 2 fan cover halves together and twist in the 4 screws on the back panel side.

![Fanbox_Step_6_2](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/fc9bd8f6-1c70-4312-8ff7-11bffc65dffb)

![Fanbox_Step_6](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/6b19402b-7c37-4852-b1b7-be2c9a517f7f)

7. Place the motor to ball bearing connector on the motor and insert the bottom of the fan lever into the ball bearing held in place by the ball bearing cover.

![Fanbox_Step_7](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/9201a689-02e2-47d7-91ad-2a14f63e5cb9)

8. Place 2 ball bearings in one half of the ball bearing to ball bearing connector, then cover with the other half and seal shut with 2 M3x8mm screws and 2 bolts.

![Fanbox_Step_8](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/ff755ad2-00d2-4499-b5f4-c13f5746ac11)

![Fanbox_Step_8_2](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/a38472ab-167d-459d-8753-1d1d6288d51f)

9. Attach a fan to the fan lever using zip ties and connect the completed ball bearing to ball bearing connector to the motor to ball bearing part and the fan lever.

![Fanbox_Step_9](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/a6a5d603-c97e-4b90-a9bb-e916e8a4cadc)
